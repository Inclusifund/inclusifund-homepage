# We Are Not Self-Made

*A manifesto for the people who know better.*

---

## I. The Lie

They stood on other people's shoulders and called it climbing.
They drank from wells they didn't dig and called it hustle.
They were born on third base and wrote books about hitting triples.

The individualist frame is the anaesthetic of the privileged.
It numbs them to the hands that raised them,
the roads that carried them,
the strangers whose labour built the chair they sit in
as they tell you that you are the author of your own outcome.

## II. The Truth

No one raised themselves.
No one taught themselves language.
No one wrote their own safety net, their own postcode, their own parents.

Every *"I did it alone"* is a forgetting.
Every *"pull yourself up"* is a boot that was handed to you.
The self-made myth is the most expensive lie ever sold to poor people —
because it convinces them their poverty is their fault,
and convinces the rich their wealth is their virtue.

## III. The Unit

The unit is not the self.
The unit is humankind.

We are a species that thinks in networks,
heals in circles,
and builds in relay.
The lone genius is a story told by people who owe everyone.

A human alone is a prototype.
A human in community is a circuit.
A community in motion is a civilisation.

## IV. What We Build

We build ecosystems, not empires.
We build circuits, not ladders.
We build floors, not ceilings.

When one of us rises, the lift must hold others.
When one of us eats, the plate must travel.
When one of us wins, the win must compound for the ones still starting.

This is not charity. This is physics.
Nothing in nature grows alone.
Forests share roots. Cells share signals. Fire shares heat.
Isolation is not strength — it is the shape of something about to die.

## V. The Invitation

If you came here to climb alone — this isn't your room.
If you came here to climb *with* — welcome home.

We are not here to make a few of us rich.
We are here to make all of us possible.

We are InclusiFund.
We rank up together.
