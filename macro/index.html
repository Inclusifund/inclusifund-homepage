Q2 fiscal-tightening squeeze. The maze is consolidating.

Spring/summer Q2 2026 is the shortest funding-decision window of the year for UK CICs — most foundation rounds close mid-May to mid-July. The Spring Statement (27 March 2026) reaffirmed fiscal-headroom constraint: "no new departmental upliftls". Existing pots get smaller envelopes for FY26-27, not bigger.

Means: don't wait for new pots. Mine the existing Tier 3 / 5 / 6 channels harder.

What's flowing right now — Tier-by-tier

  • Tier 2 (HM Treasury): Spring Statement reaffirmed fiscal-headroom constraint. Departmental budgets flat-or-declining in real terms FY26-27. No new flagship community fund announced.
  • Tier 3 (Arms-Length): National Lottery Community Fund's Awards-for-All processing time has crept from 12 to 16 weeks — apply earlier than you think. NLHF Heritage Fund's "Levelling Up for Culture" reapplications open Q3.
  • Tier 4 (Foundations): Lankelly Chase wind-down nearly complete — £15m/yr being redistributed to peer foundations through 2025–27. Tudor Trust criteria refresh due Q2.
  • Tier 5 (Corporate CSR + Banks): Tide bank still rejecting CICs as of April 2026 (verified per InclusiFund Common Standard pledge). Co-op Bank, Unity, Metro, Triodos remain CIC-open. Lloyds Bank Foundation's "Enable" closed for Q2; reopens autumn.
  • Tier 6 (Local Government): UKSPF allocations to combined authorities for Year 3 (FY26-27) being published this quarter. Single-settlement deals (GMCA, WMCA, Liverpool City Region, West Yorkshire) absorb portion of CSR-adjacent community spend.
  • Tier 7 (Aggregators): 360Giving Q1 2026 data drop pending. Once published, run a who-funded-whom matching pass against your CIC's mission keywords — that's your warm-pitch list.

What to grab this week

  1. Today: Pull your CIC's mission to 5 keywords. Search 360Giving's published Q4 2025 dataset for "CIC + [your keywords]" — list every funder who's given to a CIC like yours in the last 12 months.
  2. This week: Apply to Awards for All if you haven't in the last 12 months. Submission window is now wider than it'll be in autumn.
  3. This week: If your CIC banks with Tide — switch. Co-op or Unity. The dispute is structural, not personal.
  4. By Fri 1 May: If you're an SME/CIC in a single-settlement combined-authority area (GMCA / WMCA / Liverpool / West Yorks), check your CA's published Year 3 UKSPF priorities and write a 1-paragraph alignment statement.
  5. Strategic: Build a relationship with one Tier 4 foundation officer over the next 6 months. Worth more than 10 cold applications.

Pattern of the day

Friction is the moat. The wind-downs (Lankelly, Tudor refresh) plus the headroom-tightening (Spring Statement) plus the Tier 5 inertia (Tide) is producing a quiet squeeze. Founders who build relationships with 2–3 specific gatekeepers in 2026–27 will out-perform founders who keep applying to 30 cold pots.
