# The Receipts

*A ledger of what we move. Seven figures, true to the pound.*

---

## The seven numbers

### £1 — the single pound
One donation, one bucket, one person. Every fund — ours, theirs, the billion-pound ones — starts this way.

### £500 — a UK family of four's monthly food shop
Roughly what it costs to feed a UK family of four for a month. One pound became five hundred.

### £16,500 — a live grant
The exact sum Earth Village CIC submitted to the National Lottery last week. A full year of community programming in Nottingham.

### £1.2 million — what InclusiFund has moved so far
Into grassroots hands. On track for £3M by October 2026.

### £4.2 billion — the UK's annual small-charity funding gap
What UK small charities (income under £1M) are short of every single year. Roughly 3,500× what we move today.

### £138 billion — liquid wealth held by the UK's top 1%
Cash and shares. Thirty-three times the entire sector's annual gap. *The wealth isn't missing — it's held.*

---

## What this ledger says

The system isn't held up by scale — it's held up by the *absence of people moving*.

The £4.2bn gap is a policy failure **and** a behaviour failure. Policy because the funding architecture routes money to institutions that already have money. Behaviour because the people with liquidity haven't been asked in a way that makes moving feel like building rather than giving away.

InclusiFund's job is to make the act of moving money into grassroots hands feel like joining an ecosystem, not like filling a gap.
