The Common Standard — three tiers

Signatory banks publicly commit to one of the following. Each tier includes the tier below.

Bronze

Publish and be judged

  • Publish a named public CIC onboarding process with indicative timeline.
  • Name a regional escalation contact for stuck CICs.
  • Where CICs are not currently served, publish a roadmap to begin — with a target date.

Silver

45 days, verified

  • 45-day maximum onboarding for CICs meeting Governance Verification.
  • Quarterly publication of CIC wait-time data.
  • Grant-drawdown pre-authorisation mechanism.

Gold

21 days, staffed, reported

  • 21-day maximum onboarding for CICs meeting Governance Verification.
  • Dedicated CIC relationship team or named CIC pathway.
  • Annual public CIC banking report.
  • Grant-drawdown pre-authorisation mechanism.

Governance Verification

The Common Standard SLAs apply to CICs that meet a published governance floor. Satisfied by any of:

  • Current ORCIC filing compliance
  • A valid CIC36 approval less than 12 months old
  • Endorsement from a recognised sector body (Social Enterprise UK, Power to Change, CIC Association)

What the Common Standard does not ask

  • Relaxing KYC, AML, or sanctions screening.
  • Guaranteeing account opening — only acting within the window or escalating.
  • Underwriting CIC commercial risk.
  • Publishing customer-identifiable data.
